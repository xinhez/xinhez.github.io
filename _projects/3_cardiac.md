## Summary

Understanding how transplanted human cardiomyocytes integrate electrically into host cardiac tissue is critical for cell therapy safety — but conventional electrophysiology lacks the spatial and temporal resolution to resolve individual graft cells in a living heart. This work develops ultra-flexible nanoelectronic meshes that can be implanted alongside transplanted cells to record and map electrical activity at single-cell resolution over weeks.

**Key results:**
- Continuous, high-resolution electrical recording from transplanted human cardiomyocytes in vivo
- Identified spatial patterns of arrhythmogenesis not detectable with bulk electrophysiology
- Developed a spike classification algorithm separating graft-derived automaticity from host sinus rhythm at millisecond resolution

## Why It Matters

This platform bridges implantable bioelectronics and cell therapy, providing a direct window into how transplanted cells behave electrically over time in a living host. The findings have direct implications for designing safer cardiomyocyte transplantation therapies.
