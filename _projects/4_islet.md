## Summary

Transplanted human islet cells undergo a period of electrical maturation after engraftment, but the principles governing this process are poorly understood. This work uses chronically implanted flexible electronics to continuously record electrical activity during maturation, paired with spatial transcriptomics to link gene expression patterns to electrical phenotypes.

**Key results:**
- Long-term, stable electrical recordings from transplanted islet cells in vivo
- Linked transcriptomic state to electrical maturation trajectory at single-cell resolution
- Identified SC-α / SC-β cell classification from extracellular recordings alone

## Why It Matters

This multi-modal approach — combining chronic implantable recording with spatial omics — establishes a new paradigm for studying transplanted cell development in living tissue. Findings directly inform the design of more effective islet transplantation therapies for Type 1 diabetes.
