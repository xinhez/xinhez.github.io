## Summary

Cardiac organoid maturation is slow, expensive, and sensitive to stimulation protocols — but the parameter space is too large for exhaustive search. This work builds a closed-loop AI platform that uses Gaussian Process-based Bayesian optimization to adaptively select electrical stimulation protocols, guiding organoids toward faster and more complete maturation.

**Key results:**
- ~70% higher conduction velocity in optimized organoids versus fixed-parameter baselines
- Closed-loop design reduced the number of experiments needed to find high-performing protocols
- System integrates real-time electrophysiology readouts as the optimization feedback signal

## Why It Matters

This work demonstrates that AI-driven closed-loop experimental design can meaningfully accelerate biological research — replacing grid search with data-efficient adaptive optimization. The platform is generalizable to other biological optimization problems, including drug dosing, culture conditions, and stimulation waveforms.
